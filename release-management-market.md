<p><p>Release management encompasses three primary market types: major releases, minor releases, and emergency releases. A major release introduces significant changes or new features to a software product, often requiring extensive testing and user training. Minor releases focus on minor enhancements, bug fixes, or performance improvements with reduced risk involved. Emergency releases address urgent issues or critical bugs that must be resolved quickly to maintain system stability or security. Each type plays a crucial role in ensuring software development and operational efficiency.</p></p>
