<p><p>Fiber Reinforced Polymer Composite Rebar (FRP Rebar) is a type of reinforcement used in construction, made from a combination of polymer materials and fibers such as glass, aramid, or carbon. It is lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and offers high tensile strength, making it an increasingly popular alternative to traditional steel rebar in various construction applications. FRP rebar is particularly advantageous in environments where corrosion is a concern, extending the lifespan of structures and reducing maintenance costs.</p><p>The FRP Rebar market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.2% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for durable and sustainable construction materials. Key trends influencing this growth include advancements in manufacturing technologies, which enhance the performance characteristics of FRP rebar, and rising awareness of the benefits of using non-corrosive materials in construction. Additionally, the global push towards infrastructure development and modernization is propelling the demand for innovative building solutions. The construction sector's shift towards eco-friendly alternatives is further fostering market expansion.
