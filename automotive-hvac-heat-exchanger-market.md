<p><p>The automotive HVAC heat exchanger market includes three main types: condensers, evaporators, and heat cores. Condensers dissipate heat from refrigerants, converting them from gas to liquid, while evaporators absorb heat from the cabin air, cooling it by turning the refrigerant from liquid to gas. Heat cores, typically used in heating systems, transfer engine coolant heat to warm the cabin air. Each component plays a crucial role in regulating temperature and ensuring passenger comfort within vehicles.</p></p>
