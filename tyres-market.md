<p><p>The tire market can be broadly classified into skewed and radial tires. Skewed tires, often referred to as bias-ply tires, feature layers of fabric plies that crisscross, offering a sturdy structure, but generally less flexibility and performance at high speeds. On the other hand, radial tires have plies that run perpendicular to the direction of travel, providing enhanced stability, better fuel efficiency, and superior traction. The radial tire segment has become increasingly popular due to its performance advantages and adaptability across various vehicle types.</p></p>
