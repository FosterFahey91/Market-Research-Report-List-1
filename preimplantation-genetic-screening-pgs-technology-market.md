<p><strong>The Preimplantation Genetic Screening (PGS) Technology Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Next-generation Sequencing</li><li>Polymerase Chain Reaction</li><li>Fluorescence in Situ Hybridization</li><li>Comparative Genomic Hybridization</li><li>Single-nucleotide Polymorphism</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Preimplantation Genetic Screening (PGS) technology encompasses various market types used to assess embryos for genetic abnormalities prior to implantation. Next-generation sequencing (NGS) provides comprehensive genomic analysis, offering high throughput and accuracy. Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) amplifies specific DNA segments, enabling targeted genetic testing. Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) visualizes chromosomal abnormalities using fluorescent probes. Comparative genomic hybridization (CGH) detects copy number variations across the genome, while single-nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) analysis identifies genetic variations at specific points, assisting in selecting viable embryos.</p></p>
