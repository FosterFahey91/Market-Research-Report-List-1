<p><p>The self-lubricated bearing market is categorized into metallic and non-metallic types. Metallic self-lubricated bearings are typically made from materials like bronze, aluminum, or steel, incorporating solid lubricants to reduce friction and wear while enhancing durability in heavy-load applications. Conversely, non-metallic self-lubricated bearings utilize materials such as polymers or composites, providing low friction and corrosion resistance, making them suitable for lighter loads and environments where metal corrosion is a concern. Each type addresses specific operational needs and performance criteria.</p></p>
