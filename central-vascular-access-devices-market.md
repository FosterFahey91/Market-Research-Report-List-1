<p><strong>The Central Vascular Access Devices Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC)</li><li>Tunneled Catheters</li><li>Non Tunneled Catheters</li><li>Implanted Ports</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Central Vascular Access Devices (CVADs) are essential for administering medications and drawing blood. Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICCs) are inserted through peripheral veins, providing long-term access with reduced infection risk. Tunneled catheters are inserted surgically and lie under the skin, ideal for prolonged use. Non-tunneled catheters are typically placed for short-term access, either directly into central veins or femoral veins. Implanted ports are subcutaneously placed devices allowing for infrequent access while minimizing infection risk and patient discomfort.</p></p>
