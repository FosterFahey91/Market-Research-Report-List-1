<p><ul><li>Impregnation method</li><li>CVD method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carbon matrix composites are specialized materials used in high-temperature applications, characterized by their strong and lightweight properties. The impregnation method involves infusing a carbon precursor into a carbon fiber structure, enhancing its strength and thermal stability. In contrast, the Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) method involves depositing a carbon-rich gas onto a substrate, forming a dense matrix around the fibers. Both methods contribute to the performance and durability of composites, catering to industries like aerospace, automotive, and energy.</p></p>
