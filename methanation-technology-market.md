<p><p>Methanation technology encompasses two primary market types: CO2 conversion and CO conversion. The CO2-to-methane process utilizes captured carbon dioxide, often from industrial emissions, to produce synthetic methane through catalytic reactions. This approach offers a pathway for carbon recycling and emissions reduction. Conversely, the CO-to-methane process involves converting carbon monoxide, commonly derived from biomass gasification or syngas, into methane. Both methods contribute to renewable energy sources and support the transition to a sustainable, circular carbon economy.</p></p>
