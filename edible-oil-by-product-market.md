<p><p>The edible oil by-product market consists of two main types: liquid co-products and solid co-products. Liquid co-products typically include oils and fatty acids that can be used in food formulations, cosmetics, or biodiesel production. Solid co-products, on the other hand, include meal, cake, or pulp residues that are often utilized as animal feed or in bioenergy applications. Both types are valuable for achieving sustainable practices by minimizing waste and maximizing resource utilization in the food industry.</p></p>
