<p><p>The automotive engine coolant market comprises several types, primarily focusing on ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is the most widely used coolant due to its effective heat transfer and antifreeze properties, although it is toxic. Propylene glycol serves as a safer alternative, being less harmful while still providing adequate cooling and freeze protection. Glycerin is a biodegradable option, appealing for eco-friendly applications but less common in conventional uses. Each type offers distinct benefits tailored to various automotive needs.</p></p>
