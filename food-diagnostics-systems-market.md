<p><p>The Food Diagnostics Systems market is primarily segmented into four types: Chromatography, Spectrometry, Biosensors, and Immunoassays. Chromatography techniques separate components within food samples for analysis, while Spectrometry measures the mass and concentration of substances. Biosensors utilize biological molecules to detect pathogens and toxins, providing rapid results. Immunoassays employ antigen-antibody reactions to identify specific contaminants or proteins, ensuring food safety and quality. Each method plays a crucial role in enhancing food diagnostics and ensuring regulatory compliance.</p></p>
