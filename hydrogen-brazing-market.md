<p><p>Hydrogen brazing is a joining process that utilizes hydrogen as a reducing atmosphere to enhance bonding. The market is segmented into two main types: dry hydrogen brazing and wet hydrogen brazing. Dry hydrogen brazing involves a controlled, moisture-free environment, ideal for achieving high-strength joints in sensitive applications. In contrast, wet hydrogen brazing incorporates a slight presence of moisture, enhancing fluidity and allowing for better flow of filler materials. Both methods cater to diverse industrial needs, including aerospace, automotive, and electronics.</p></p>
