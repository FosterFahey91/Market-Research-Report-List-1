<p><p>The rice starch market is categorized into two primary types: food grade rice starch and industry grade rice starch. Food grade rice starch is used in the food industry as a thickening agent, stabilizer, and texturizer, essential for various products such as sauces, puddings, and snacks. In contrast, industry grade rice starch serves non-food applications, including adhesives, paper manufacturing, and textiles, where it acts as a binder or filler, contributing to the performance and quality of various industrial products.</p></p>
